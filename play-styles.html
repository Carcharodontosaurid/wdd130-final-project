Finding Your Style

Did you know there is more than one style of yoyo? Some are probably more familiar to you (such as responsive and looping), and others may be more suprising (like 3A or Freehand). But we're here to walk you through each one to find your perfect style.

1a (Looping)

This is probably what most people think of when they think of yoyo. This is the most common and most versitile style on this list. Most tricks involve loops and landing the yoyo back on sections of string and 'hopping' them between different configurations. Some of these are done to make pictures of the string, aptly named "picture tricks." Rock the Baby and Eiffiel tower are great examples of these.

2a (Two Handed)

In 2a, each hand has a yoyo connected to it and performs it's own tricks and loops. While it can be a very challenging style to learn, it is also one of the most impressive and awe-inspiring.

3a (Two Handed)

Like 2a, in 3a each hand has a yoyo connected to it. But here the two yoyos interact with each other to perform far more complex, and by extention, incredible tricks. Also like 2a, it is both a difficult and rewarding style to learn.

4a (Off-string)

4a, or "Offstring", is a style of yoyoing where the string is not connected to the yoyo at all. As you can probably guess, this allows for some very unique tricks and techniques that can only be accomplished in 4a. It's actually one of the easier styles to learn, and can be a viable option for a beginner to learn.
